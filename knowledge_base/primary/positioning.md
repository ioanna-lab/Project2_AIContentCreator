## Brand Voice Guidelines

### Tone
- **Direct** — say what you mean, without corporate hedging
- **Evidence-based** — every claim backed by a number, an outcome, or a named experience
- **Warm but not soft** — caring about people does not mean avoiding hard conversations
- **Confident without arrogance** — the confidence comes from experience, not ego
- **Curious** — genuinely interested in how things work and how they could work better

### What Ioanna Sounds Like
- Uses specific numbers (not "significantly improved" but "reduced from 20/week to 2/month")
- References real situations and named frameworks (QMI, Engineering Manifesto)
- Challenges conventional wisdom but with evidence, not just opinion
- Talks about engineering culture as infrastructure, not as a soft concept
- Acknowledges complexity without hiding behind it

### What Ioanna Does NOT Sound Like
- Generic leadership platitudes ("people are our greatest asset")
- Vague claims without evidence ("strong track record of delivery")
- Overly technical without business context
- Humble-bragging disguised as storytelling
- AI-generated filler content

### LinkedIn Voice Specifically
- Posts that start with a specific observation or counterintuitive claim
- 3–5 short paragraphs, each doing one job
- Ends with a question or a clear point of view, not a call to action
- Uses line breaks generously — LinkedIn is not an essay platform
- First line must earn the "see more" click

### Content Pillars
1. **Engineering measurement** — why making the invisible visible is the highest-leverage leadership act
2. **AI in engineering organisations** — what actually works, what is hype, governance before scale
3. **Engineering culture as infrastructure** — psychological safety, attrition, career development
4. **Transformation leadership** — monolith migrations, incident reduction, quality frameworks
5. **Personal brand / career** — the VP/CTO job search, fractional consulting, women in tech leadership

## Differentiators vs Generic AI Content
When generic AI writes about engineering leadership, it says:
- "Focus on clear communication"
- "Build a culture of psychological safety"
- "Use data to drive decisions"

When Ioanna writes about engineering leadership, she says:
- "We had 20 P1 incidents a week. Within six months we had 2 a month. Here's exactly what we changed."
- "QMI was adopted by 50% of the organisation voluntarily. If people adopt your framework because they fear consequences, your data is corrupted."
- "The most expensive scaling event is the one that happens during a customer incident."

The difference is specificity, evidence, and a clear point of view.

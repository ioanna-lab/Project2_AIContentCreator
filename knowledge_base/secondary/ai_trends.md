# AI Trends in Engineering Leadership — Secondary Research

## Current State of AI in Engineering Organisations (2025-2026)

### Adoption Patterns
- AI coding assistants (GitHub Copilot, Cursor, Claude Code) are now standard in most scale-up engineering organisations
- The differentiation is no longer "do you use AI tools" but "how well do you govern and scale AI adoption"
- Organisations that adopted AI tools without governance frameworks are now dealing with security incidents, quality regressions, and inconsistent practices
- The most advanced organisations are building internal AI platforms rather than relying on point tools

### The Governance Gap
- Most engineering organisations have AI tools deployed but lack formal governance
- Common problems: shadow AI usage, inconsistent prompt practices, security risks from uncontrolled API usage
- The organisations winning are those who treated AI adoption like they treated cloud adoption in 2012 — with infrastructure thinking, not just tool procurement

### AI in Platform Engineering
- Platform teams are increasingly being asked to provide AI capabilities as internal services
- The "platform as a product" model is extending to AI tooling — internal teams as customers of AI infrastructure
- MCP (Model Context Protocol) emerging as a standard for connecting LLMs to internal tools and data

### The Skills Gap
- Senior engineers who understand both LLM capabilities and production systems are extremely scarce
- Most "AI engineers" have either deep ML background or software engineering background, rarely both
- Engineering leaders who can bridge the gap between AI capabilities and production engineering are in high demand

## Engineering Leadership Market Trends (2025-2026)

### What Companies Are Looking For in VP/CTO Candidates
- Experience with AI-native development practices (not just AI-assisted)
- Track record of building platform engineering capabilities
- Ability to navigate the build/buy/borrow decision for AI tooling
- Evidence of governance frameworks, not just adoption
- Cross-functional influence (working with Product, Data, Security simultaneously)

### The Measurement Revolution
- DORA metrics are now table stakes — companies expect engineering leaders to know them
- The next frontier is AI-specific metrics: AI code contribution rates, AI-introduced defect rates, prompt quality metrics
- Engineering leaders who can define new measurement frameworks for AI-augmented teams are rare and valued

### Remote and Hybrid Engineering Leadership
- The best engineering talent in Europe is distributed
- Companies that insist on full-time office are losing talent to remote-first competitors
- Engineering leaders who can build culture and maintain quality across distributed teams are premium

### Platform Engineering as Strategic Differentiator
- Platform engineering is moving from cost centre to strategic capability
- Companies with strong internal platforms ship faster, hire better, and retain longer
- The ROI of platform engineering investment is increasingly understood at board level

## Key Industry Reports and Data Points

### Developer Productivity
- Teams using AI coding assistants report 20-40% productivity improvements for routine tasks
- Complex architectural and systems thinking tasks show minimal AI productivity improvement
- The productivity gains are unevenly distributed — junior engineers benefit more than senior ones

### Incident Management
- Mean Time to Detect (MTTD) and Mean Time to Resolve (MTTR) remain the key operational metrics
- AI-assisted incident detection is reducing MTTD by 30-50% in leading organisations
- Post-mortem automation (AI-generated summaries from incident data) is emerging as a time saver

### Technical Debt
- Studies consistently show 20-40% of engineering capacity consumed by technical debt in organisations without explicit debt management
- The 60/40 model (60% features, 40% platform/debt) is gaining adoption as a deliberate policy
- AI tools are creating new categories of technical debt (prompt debt, model versioning debt)

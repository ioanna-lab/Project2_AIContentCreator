# CTO/VP Engineering Market Landscape — Secondary Research

## The European Engineering Leadership Market (2025-2026)

### Demand Signals
- Series B/C companies scaling from 20 to 100+ engineers are the highest-demand segment for VP Engineering roles
- Berlin, Amsterdam, and London remain the three largest European engineering leadership markets
- Remote-first companies are increasingly competitive with on-site Berlin/Amsterdam roles on compensation
- CTO roles at AI-native startups command significant premium over traditional software companies

### Compensation Benchmarks (European Market)
- VP Engineering at Series B: €180k–€250k base + equity
- VP Engineering at Series C/D: €220k–€300k base + equity
- CTO at AI-native startup: €200k–€350k base + significant equity
- Fractional CTO: €800–€1,500/day depending on engagement scope

### What Hiring Managers Actually Screen For
1. **Transformation track record** — not just "managed large teams" but "changed how the team works"
2. **Business outcome connection** — engineers who speak revenue and retention, not just uptime
3. **AI capability** — understanding of LLMs, AI tooling governance, not just "we used Copilot"
4. **Cultural builder** — evidence of retention, career development, psychological safety outcomes
5. **Cross-functional credibility** — can work with Product, Data, Finance at peer level

### Common Interview Failures for Senior Engineering Leaders
- Too technical, not enough strategic altitude
- Can't connect engineering decisions to business outcomes
- No evidence of building culture, only building systems
- Vague about actual impact ("led a team of 200" without outcomes)
- No framework for AI adoption — seen as behind the curve

## Fractional CTO Market

### Growing Demand Segments
- Pre-Series A startups that need CTO-level thinking without full-time cost
- Series A companies between permanent CTOs (transition periods)
- Traditional businesses undergoing digital transformation
- Companies building AI capabilities without internal expertise

### What Fractional Clients Need
- Fast context acquisition (able to diagnose quickly)
- Network to fill gaps (engineering hiring, vendor selection)
- Governance frameworks that work at small scale
- Honest assessment of technical debt and risk

### Platforms and Channels
- Malt (European freelance platform) — strong for senior tech profiles
- CTO Craft community — peer network and opportunity source
- LinkedIn — primary channel for inbound fractional opportunities
- Direct referrals — most high-value engagements come through network
